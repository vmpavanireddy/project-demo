Types of gaming are broadly categorized bygenre, like Action, RPG, Strategy, Simulation, Sports, Puzzle, and Adventure, often blended (Action-Adventure, RPG Shooter), focusing on gameplay (FPS, Platformer, Battle Royale) or player engagement (Sandbox, MMO, Casual, Party). Games can also be classified by platform (PC, Console, Mobile) or monetization (Free-to-Play).
By Gameplay & Genre:

Action: Focuses on physical challenges, reflexes, and combat (e.g., Shooters, Fighting, Beat 'em ups).
RPG Deep storytelling, character development, and fantasy worlds (e.g., JRPG, MMORPG, Action RPG).
Strategy: Emphasizes planning and tactical thinking (e.g., RTS, Tactical RPG).
Simulation: Replicates real-world activities or systems (e.g., Flight Sim, Life Sim, City Builders).
Adventure: Focuses on exploration, puzzles, and narrative.
Sports & Racing: Simulates real-world sports or vehicle competitions (e.g., Football, Kart Racing, Sim Racing).
Puzzle: Challenges logic and problem-solving skills.
Platformer: Navigating obstacles and platforms Super Mario.
Survival & Horror: Focuses on resource management, tension, and suspense.

Classifications:
Platform: PC, Console (PlayStation, Xbox, Switch), Mobile, VR.
Monetization: Free-to-Play (F2P), Premium (Paid), Subscription.
